Spectral Subtraction Filtering

For something as delicate as a voice recognition algorithm, you want the input data to be as clean and consistent as possible. In other words, the optimal piece of software would be able to recognize a given voice regardless of whether it was recorded in a noisy hallway or an empty room.

Spectral subtraction is a method to remove such background noise without damaging useful data from the speech itself. Its basic principle can be derived from its name: it involves generating a frequency spectrum from a sample of background noise – “spectral” – then subtracts this frequency profile from the sample as a whole – “subtraction.” Under the assumption that the original recording was some combination of clean voice data and a noisy environment, you are able to replicate a clean recording by subtracting out the extraneous noise in this way.
