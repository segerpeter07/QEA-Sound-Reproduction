Linear Regression

As an alternative to eigenvoices, we experimented with linear regression as a way to identify individual's voices. In this method, rather than projecting the data into high-variation orthonormal bases, you solve a system of equations for your training data. In our case, we used a downsampled discrete Fourier transform of each voice sample.

We also included a regularization coefficient to our equation. This term helps prevent the regression model from becoming overconstrained to the training data and giving unreliable results. To optimize this constant for accuracy in this particular situation, we tested several different constants with our training data and calculated an average accuracy. This resulted in a clear peak around 2500, from which we can get an accuracy of over 36%.
